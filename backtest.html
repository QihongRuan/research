💡 Key Findings

Small + Value = Best Factors

Small Cap L/S (SR 1.87) and Value L/S (SR 1.94) are the strongest standalone factors over 26 years. Combining them with momentum in a multi-factor composite yields SR 1.98 — the best risk-adjusted strategy.

Equal-Weight >> Value-Weight

Equal-weighted market (18.3% ann.) dramatically outperforms value-weighted (9.9%). This ~8% gap reflects the small-cap premium embedded in equal weighting — effectively a free alpha source.

Momentum Crashed in the US

Pure momentum L/S lost -97% over 26 years. The 2009 momentum crash was devastating, and the factor never recovered. However, momentum remains effective in other markets (e.g., A-shares).

Market Neutral Doesn't Work (US)

Market neutral returns -5.2% annually in the US. Contrast with Chinese private funds achieving 6-7% — reflecting A-share market's greater pricing inefficiency and retail investor participation.

Quality = Ultimate Ballast

Quality factor shows only 2.3% annual return but with just 2.8% volatility and -2.8% max drawdown. SR 0.81 makes it the ideal portfolio stabilizer and risk-off allocation.

2025: Value Renaissance

Value L/S returned +42.0% YTD in 2025, and Multi-Factor surged +65.6%. The value rotation that began post-COVID continues to deliver, potentially driven by higher interest rates favoring cheap stocks.

📋 Methodology

Data

  • Source: WRDS CRSP v2 (crsp_a_stock.msf_v2)
  • Universe: NYSE (N), AMEX (A), NASDAQ (Q) — common equity only (sharetype='NS', securitytype='EQTY')
  • Period: January 2000 – December 2025 (311 formation months)
  • Minimum price: $1 (penny stock filter)
  • Factors: Fama-French 5 Factors + Momentum (from ff.fivefactors_daily)

Signal Construction

  • Momentum (12-2): Cumulative return from month t-12 to t-2, skipping most recent month
  • Value: Inverse price (1/P) as book-to-market proxy
  • Size: Log market capitalization
  • Volatility: Trailing 12-month return standard deviation
  • Reversal: Prior 1-month return
  • Multi-Factor: Z-score composite: 40% Momentum + 30% Value + 30% Low Volatility

Portfolio Construction

  • Rebalance: Monthly (end of month)
  • Weighting: Equal-weight within quintiles
  • Long-Short: Long top quintile (Q5), short bottom quintile (Q1)
  • Long-biased strategies: Various net exposure profiles (see table)
  • Transaction costs: Not included (gross returns)
  • Minimum stocks per month: 200

Limitations

  • No transaction costs, slippage, or market impact modeled
  • Equal-weight portfolios may overweight illiquid micro-caps
  • 1/P is a rough value proxy — proper book-to-market requires Compustat merge
  • Survivorship bias partially mitigated by CRSP delisting-inclusive returns
  • Results are before fees; actual fund returns would be 2-5% lower annually
