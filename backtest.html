Key Findings

Value + Small Cap Dominate

Small Cap L/S (SR 1.87) and Value L/S (SR 1.94) are the strongest standalone factors. Their composite with momentum yields SR 1.98.

Equal-Weight Premium

Equal-weighted market returns 18.3% vs 9.9% value-weighted. The 8.4% gap is the small-cap premium embedded in equal weighting.

Momentum Is Dead (in the US)

Pure momentum L/S lost 97% over 26 years. The 2009 crash was fatal and the factor never recovered. Remains effective in other markets.

Market Neutral Fails

Returns −5.2% annually in the US. Chinese private funds achieve 6–7% — reflecting greater pricing inefficiency in A-shares.

Quality as Ballast

Only 2.3% annual return but 2.8% volatility and −2.8% max drawdown. SR 0.81 makes it the ideal portfolio stabilizer.

2025: Value Renaissance

Value L/S +42.0% YTD. Multi-Factor surged +65.6%. The post-COVID value rotation continues, likely driven by higher rates.

Methodology

Data

  • Source: WRDS CRSP v2 (crsp_a_stock.msf_v2), through December 2025
  • Universe: NYSE, AMEX, NASDAQ — common equity (sharetype=NS, securitytype=EQTY)
  • Factors: Fama-French 5 + Momentum (ff.fivefactors_daily)
  • Price filter: >$1 (exclude penny stocks)

Signals

  • Momentum (12-2): Cumulative return t−12 to t−2, skip recent month
  • Value: Inverse price as B/M proxy
  • Size: Log market capitalization
  • Volatility: Trailing 12-month return std dev
  • Reversal: Prior 1-month return
  • Multi-Factor: Z-score: 40% Mom + 30% Value + 30% Low Vol

Portfolio Construction

  • Monthly rebalance, end of month
  • Equal-weight within quintile portfolios
  • L/S: Long Q5, short Q1
  • Minimum 200 stocks per formation month
  • No transaction costs (gross returns)

Limitations

  • No transaction costs, slippage, or market impact
  • Equal-weight may overweight illiquid micro-caps
  • 1/P is a rough value proxy; proper B/M requires Compustat
  • Survivorship bias partially mitigated by CRSP delisting returns
  • Actual fund returns would be 2–5% lower after fees
