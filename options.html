Overview

Systematic backtest of two options selling strategies — Covered Call and Cash-Secured Put — on seven major US equities using OptionMetrics data from WRDS. The analysis covers 18.7 million option records across 2020–2025, with 553 trades per strategy.

Methodology

Data: WRDS OptionMetrics (optionm.opprcd2020 through opprcd2025), 18,665,143 records
Universe: AAPL, AMZN, GOOG, META, MSFT, NVDA, TSLA
Entry: Monthly (~every 25 calendar days)
Option selection: ~30 DTE, delta ≈ 0.30 (calls) / −0.30 (puts)
Pricing: Mid-market (best_bid + best_offer) / 2
Exit: Hold to expiration
Covered Call P&L: Stock return + premium yield − max(0, ST−K) / S0
Cash-Secured Put P&L: Premium yield − max(0, K−ST) / K

I. Covered Call Strategy

Buy-and-hold the stock while selling monthly OTM calls (delta ~0.30). This strategy generates income from premium collection while capping upside at the strike price.

II. Cash-Secured Put Strategy

Sell monthly OTM puts (delta ~−0.30) while holding cash to cover potential assignment. Premium collected as income; risk is buying the stock at the strike price during drawdowns.

The strategy collects 2.53% in premium but loses catastrophically during the ~19% of months when stocks decline sharply. The asymmetry of option payoffs (small frequent gains vs. large rare losses) makes this a negative expected value proposition for most stocks.

Recommendation

1. Covered Call on MSFT (SR 1.00): The optimal options selling strategy. Low IV, steady uptrend, moderate assignment rate.

2. Avoid Cash-Secured Puts on high-beta stocks (TSLA, NVDA, COIN): Premium is high but tail risk destroys cumulative returns.

3. Regime filter critical: Both strategies need a volatility regime filter — sell options in low-vol periods, reduce exposure when VIX spikes above 25.
